About Me

Hello! I'm Pooya Alehossein. I'm a third-year student studying Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics at George Mason University. I'm passionate about tech, creativity, and the places they intersect. I love working in multidisciplinary teams, and I believe my biggest strength lies in collaborating with engineers, artists, and other creatives to bring ideas to life. I find a lot of energy in teamwork and the creative process, whether it's developing a game or solving a complex problem. I'm a Learning Assistant for MATH 213 (Calculus III), where I get to help students learn and understand calculus.

Outside the classroom, I enjoy building PCs, jamming to electronic music, watching esports, and exploring photography. Lately, I've been getting more into AI and how it can be used to build smarter systems and maybe even change how we make and play games. If you're curious about my work or want to talk tech, video games, or anything else, feel free to reach out through the contact tab!

We are Ducked

Group project for CS325 (Game Design) at George Mason University. Two-player cooperative survival game. Stranded on a sinking island known for its unpredictable weather, two ducks must scavenge for resources, build shelter, and survive escalating storms until rescue arrives. Random events—including rescuing ducklings, avoiding wildlife, and solving environmental puzzles—keep players engaged as they work together under pressure to make it out alive.

Tech: Unity, C#
